Really? Yet another MIDI inpainting paper? Yes! Sure, MIDI inpainting's been demonstated many times, but can we make it easier to do, and more expressive? Yes!

In this study, we get comparable performance to prior methods using a Hierarchical Diffusion Transformer (HDiT) that makes things simple and easy, and handles big images (long sequences) too! And we can inpaint unique shapes for our melodies and accompaniments.
But why merely solo piano MIDI??

The point of this study is not piano or MIDI per se, it's about exploring how to control generative music models. Transformer-based approaches to music modeling offer a limited suite of user control opportuntities compared to diffusion models. This paper is an early exploration of the simple idea of, "what if we took advantage of the prolific work done controllable image diffusion methods and applied them toward music generation?"
